Dashboard Help

Overview

The Dashboard is your quick financial snapshot. It shows your cash/assets, debt, net worth, envelope money, and current roadmap mission.

What this page does

It pulls totals from Accounts, Budget, Activity, and Roadmap so you can see the big picture without opening every tab.

Need More Help: How to read each section

Assets = total of accounts marked Asset.

Total Debt = high-interest debt plus low-interest debt.

Available = total money currently assigned inside envelopes.

Current Mission = the next financial priority.

Net Worth = assets minus debt.

Current Paycheck Envelopes = a quick view of envelopes tied to the current paycheck planner group.

Money Mission Roadmap (MMR)

Roadmap Help

Overview

The Money Mission Roadmap (MMR) helps you decide where your next dollar should go while keeping your progress tied to accounts, envelopes, debts, and savings goals.

What this page does

Money Mission shows your current mission, locks later missions until earlier missions are complete, and tracks debt or linked savings automatically when possible.

Need More Help: How to fill out each section

Dollar Goal = enter the target amount you want saved. Current amount updates from linked envelope available balances.

Percent Goal = enter your target percent and current percent manually.

Debt Payoff Goal = add debt accounts in Accounts. High-cost and lower-priority debt missions update automatically from those balances.

MMR Personal Information = found in Settings. Annual income and employer match help calculate roadmap retirement targets.

Income

Paychecks and planning

Income Help

Overview

The Income tab is for expected paychecks and paycheck planning.

What this page does

It groups monthly envelopes by paycheck, includes assigned flexible N/A envelopes, and shows planning-only calculations.

Need More Help: How to read it

Expected Amount = planning-only paycheck estimate. It does not add real income or change accounts.

Suggested Grouping = envelopes expected to belong with each paycheck.

Flexible Envelopes = repeating N/A monthly envelopes you manually assign to a paycheck.

One-Time Items = paycheck-only planning expenses that do not repeat and do not create permanent Budget envelopes.

Remaining = expected paycheck minus the suggested and flexible envelope totals.

Paycheck Planner

Expected paychecks are planning only. They do not increase income, available money, to be assigned, envelopes, transactions, or account balances.

Budget

Envelope budgeting

Budget Help

Overview

The Budget page uses envelopes. Each envelope is a place where money is assigned for a bill, spending category, or savings goal.

What this page does

Monthly Envelopes are for recurring monthly expenses. Savings Envelopes are for money you want to keep building over time.

Need More Help: How to fill out each field

Envelope Name = the name of the bill, spending category, or savings goal. Example: Mortgage, Groceries, Emergency Fund, Vacation.

Envelope Type = choose Monthly Envelope for recurring monthly expenses. Choose Savings Envelope for longer-term savings goals.

Required Amount = the amount needed for that monthly envelope each month.

Available Amount = the money currently assigned to that envelope right now.

Due Date = the day of the month the monthly bill is due. This helps monthly envelopes sort by due date.

Optional Goal Amount = the total savings target for a savings envelope.

Optional Target Date = the date you hope to complete the savings goal.

MMR Link = connects the envelope to a roadmap mission, such as deductibles, emergency fund, or future goals.

Pin Important Envelope = keeps an important envelope near the top of its section.

Auto Draft = choose Yes if the bill automatically pulls from your account on or near the due date. This is planning information only.

Start New Month = resets Monthly Envelopes only. Savings Envelopes do not reset. This button is now in Settings under Monthly Envelope Reset.

Activity

Income, expenses, and transfers

Activity Help

Overview

Activity records money coming in, money going out, and money moving between accounts.

What this page does

Transactions update account balances, envelope available amounts, income totals, and recent transaction history.

Need More Help: How to fill out each field

Expense = money leaving an envelope. Choose this for bills, groceries, gas, dining out, or other spending.

Income = money entering an account. Choose this for paychecks, deposits, refunds, or extra income.

Transfer = money moving from one account to another without changing income or expenses.

Card Purchase = spending on a credit card. It increases the card balance. Envelope is optional.

Card Payment = paying a credit card. It lowers the payment account and credit card balance. Envelope is optional for budgeted/minimum payments.

Amount = the dollar amount of the transaction.

Date = the transaction date. If left blank, today is used.

From Account = the account money leaves for a transfer.

To Account = the account money enters for a transfer.

Deposit Account = the account income is deposited into.

Account = required for every transaction. Choose the account that actually changed.

Envelope = optional. Choose an envelope only when the transaction should also change that envelope. Choose N/A for account-only activity.

Payee / Description = the transaction name, such as Walmart, Paycheck, Mortgage, or Transfer to Savings.

Notes = optional details you want to remember.

Accounts

Assets and debt grouped by bank

Accounts Help

Overview

Accounts are where money lives or where debt is owed. Accounts are grouped by bank on the Accounts page.

What this page does

This page tracks assets, high-interest debt, and low-interest debt. These balances feed Dashboard, Roadmap, transfers, and envelope tracking.

Need More Help: How to fill out each field

Bank Name = the financial institution. Example: Navy Federal, Capital One, Chase, Wells Fargo. Accounts with the same bank name group together.

Account Name = the type/name of the account. Example: Checking, Savings, Visa, Mortgage, Emergency Fund.

Balance = current account balance. For debt accounts, enter the amount still owed.

Account Type = Asset for money you own, High-Interest Debt for expensive debt, or Low-Interest Debt for lower-rate debt.

Is this a credit card? = choose Yes for credit cards so Money Mission can use card purchases and card payments correctly.

Pin Important Account = keeps an important account near the top of its bank group.

Up / Down arrows = move an account only within the same bank group and account type.

Settings

Data tools and MMR personal information

Settings Help

Overview

Settings controls data safety, backups, imports, exports, migrations, reset tools, monthly envelope reset, and MMR personal information.

What this page does

Money Mission is local-first. Your data stays in this browser/device unless you export it or move it yourself.

Need More Help: How to use each section

Export Data = downloads a backup JSON file. Use this before updating, changing phones, or making big edits.

Import Data = restores a Money Mission backup file and replaces current app data on this device.

Automatic Backups = stores recent snapshots locally after changes.

Restore Latest Backup = restores the newest automatic backup stored on this device.

Run Migration Again = checks older storage keys and brings older Money Mission data into the current version.

Monthly Envelope Reset = starts a new month by resetting Monthly Envelopes only.

Reset App Data = clears Money Mission data from this device.

Annual Gross Income = yearly income before taxes, used for MMR retirement calculations.

Employer Match % = the percent needed to receive the full employer retirement match.

Data Safety

V9.10.0 uses permanent storage keys and automatic backups so future updates should not erase accounts, envelopes, transactions, MMR progress, or settings.
